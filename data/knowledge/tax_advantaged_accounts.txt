Tax-Advantaged Retirement and Investment Accounts (2024-2025)

401(k): Employee limit $23,500 (2025). Catch-up (50+): additional $7,500. Always contribute enough for the full employer match. Traditional = pre-tax contributions, taxed on withdrawal. Roth 401(k) = after-tax, tax-free withdrawals.

IRA: Limit $7,000/year. Catch-up (50+): additional $1,000. Traditional = may be deductible, taxed on withdrawal. Roth = after-tax, tax-free growth. Roth income limits (2025): $150K single, $236K married.

529 Plans: Tax-free growth for qualified education expenses. Many states offer tax deductions. No federal limit but gift tax exclusion applies ($18K/year per beneficiary). Unused funds can roll into Roth IRA (up to $35K lifetime). Covers K-12 ($10K/year cap) and college.

HSA: Triple tax advantage — deductible contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free medical withdrawals. 2025 limits: $4,300 individual, $8,550 family. After 65, non-medical withdrawals taxed as income. Requires HDHP.

Priority order: 1) 401(k) to match, 2) HSA if eligible, 3) Roth IRA, 4) Max 401(k), 5) 529 for education, 6) Taxable brokerage.
