Common Investment Mistakes

1. Too much cash: Inflation erodes ~3%/year. Cash beyond emergency fund is a drag on long-term returns.

2. Market timing: Missing the 10 best days over 20 years can halve returns. Dollar-cost averaging eliminates timing risk.

3. Home country bias: US investors over-allocate domestically. US is ~60% of global market cap — diversify internationally.

4. Chasing performance: Past returns don't predict future returns. Reversion to mean is strong.

5. High fees: Active funds charge 0.5-1.5% vs 0.03-0.10% for index funds. Over 30 years, 1% fee difference on $500K costs $300K+.

6. Emotional decisions: Fear drives selling at bottoms, greed at tops. Written investment policy helps maintain discipline.

7. Tax inefficiency: Hold tax-inefficient investments (bonds, REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts. Asset location matters as much as allocation.

8. Starting late: $10K at age 25 → ~$175K by 65 (7.5% real). Same at 45 → ~$42K. Compounding rewards early action.
